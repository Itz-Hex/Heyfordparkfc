Purpose of this document
This brief sets out the proposed page structure, navigation, and content layout for the redesigned
HPFC website.

sections it should contain.
Key priorities are surfacing the three most common visitor needs — joining the club, finding our

1. Project Overview
Heyford Park FC is a youth football club founded in 2019, offering boys and girls football from
ages 4 to 16. The club has over 200 registered players and operates on core values of
Teamwork, Respect, and Inspire.
The current website (www.heyfordparkfootballclub.co.uk) covers the right ground but does not
prioritise the most common visitor journeys. This redesign addresses that by restructuring the
navigation and giving key pages more prominent placement and richer content.
1.1 Why we are redesigning
• The most important visitor journeys are buried or not clearly signposted
• "Waiting List" is an off-putting name — parents do not know whether there is space for
their child
• Locations and directions are not easy to find, despite being a top need on match days
and for new families
• The Join Us journey lacks key information (training schedule, fees, what is covered) that
would answer parents' questions upfront
1.2 Guiding principle
Every parent visiting for the first time is asking four questions in this order:
1. Can my child play here?
2. When does it happen, and where?
3. How much does it cost?
4. Who do I talk to?
The redesigned site — especially the Join Us page — should answer all four clearly and in that order.

3.4 Join Us [renamed from Waiting List]
This is the most important page on the site.
It replaces the existing Waiting List page and should be completely rewritten to answer the four
questions every prospective parent has: Can my child play here? When and where? How much does
it cost? Who do I contact?
Join Us [PRIORITY PAGE]
Purpose Convert interested parents into registrations by answering all their
questions in one place
Section 1 — Can my
child play?
Explain the age groups available (U7–U16), that the club is open to boys
and girls, and any current availability per team. Be honest if some teams
have waiting lists — it builds trust.
Section 2 — Training
schedule
Full table or card layout showing each team's training day, time, and
location. This helps parents check whether their child can actually attend
before they bother registering. Mirrors the Teams page.
Section 3 — Fees &
what's included
Subscription cost per season/month, and a clear list of what that covers
(training, match fees, kit contribution, insurance, etc.). Parents need this to
make a decision.
Section 4 — How to
join
Step-by-step process: express interest via form, club makes contact, trial
session, registration. Keep it simple and friendly.
Registration form Embedded form: parent name, child name, child date of birth (to determine
age group), preferred contact method, any notes. Submitting adds them to
the relevant team's list.
Section 5 — Contact Direct contact details for the person handling new member enquiries.
Name, email, and ideally a phone number or Facebook Messenger link. Do
not send people to the general Contact Us page from here.
Suggested page heading structure:
• H1: Join Heyford Park FC
• H2: Is there a place for my child?
• H2: When and where do teams train?
• H2: What does it cost?
• H2: How to register
• H2: Get in touch

3.5 Our Locations [new page]
This is a new page split out from Contact Us. It serves a completely different purpose — parents
and visitors navigating to a pitch on match day, or new families finding out where training takes
place.
Our Locations [NEW PAGE]
Purpose Help anyone find Heyford Park FC's grounds and training venues quickly,
including on mobile on match day
Location cards One card per venue the club uses. Each card: venue name, full address,
embedded Google Map, parking notes, and which teams use it
Directions Brief directions for each venue (from the nearest town or road junction). A
'Get directions' button linking to Google Maps is sufficient for most users.
Mobile priority This page will often be accessed on a phone while driving or walking. Maps
must load well on mobile and the address must be easily copyable or
tappable to open in a navigation app.
Pitch-finding notes If a ground has multiple pitches or an unusual entrance, add a short note
('look for the red gate', '3G pitch is at the far end of the car park', etc.)

4. Design Notes
4.1 Colour and branding
• The club's primary colour is green. Use this for key CTAs, headings, and accent
elements.
• Keep the existing club badge/logo. It should appear in the header on every page.
• The site should feel welcoming to families with young children — avoid anything that
feels overly corporate or cold.
• White backgrounds with green accents and photography of the teams will give the right
feel.

4.2 Mobile experience
• A significant proportion of visits will come from mobile, particularly from parents on
match day looking for locations or checking training times.
• The navigation should collapse to a hamburger menu on mobile.
• The Join Us page and Our Locations page in particular should be tested carefully on
small screens.
• Map embeds should be tap-friendly and link to a navigation app.
4.3 Photography
• The current site has good team photography. This should be used throughout the
redesign.
• Hero images on the home page and team cards benefit from real match/training photos
rather than stock imagery.
• Ensure any photos of children are only used with appropriate consent.
